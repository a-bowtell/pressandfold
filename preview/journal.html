What's inside

Two questions
per day.
Enough space to breathe.

The journal follows a simple morning and evening structure. Each prompt is crafted to invite noticing, not performing.

Morning Prompt — Day 1

Awareness & Grounding

Before the day asks anything of you — what do you already notice about how you feel?

Evening Prompt — Day 1

Awareness & Grounding

What was one moment today that asked for your full attention? What made it feel real?

Weekly Reflection

Closing the week

Looking back — not to judge, but to see clearly — what would you like to carry forward?

Seven themes

A journal that
moves with you.

Rather than a linear program, the journal follows seven emotional themes — cycling gently across the 30 days to create depth and variety without monotony.

I

🌱

Awareness & Grounding

Building the fundamental skill of noticing — what's present, what feels alive, what is asking for your attention.

II

✨

Curiosity & Spark

Following what genuinely interests you — not what you think should interest you. Where is your energy right now?

III

🌸

Softness & Self-Compassion

Turning toward difficulty without turning it into criticism. Being with yourself the way you'd be with someone you care about.

IV

🌤

Perspective & Pause

Stepping back from the noise. Seeing the same situations from a slightly wider angle.

V

🤝

Connection & Belonging

Reflecting on your relationships — with people, with places, and with yourself.

VI

🔆

Light & Lens

Finding the small moments of brightness. What has been good, even quietly?

VII

🌊

Closure & Continuity

Gently closing loops. Acknowledging what's done and carrying forward what matters.

What this journal is not

We want to be
honest with you.

The Intentions Journal is a structured reflection tool. It is not therapy, not a transformation guarantee, and not a productivity system. Outcomes vary. Context matters.

What it is: a quiet, consistent practice of turning toward yourself. That's enough.

This journal offers
  • Structured reflection
  • Emotional clarity
  • A consistent practice
  • Honest self-inquiry
  • Spacious prompts
  • Realistic agency
This journal does not promise
  • Healing or therapy
  • Guaranteed transformation
  • Productivity gains
  • Fixed outcomes
  • Control of your life
  • Permanent change
